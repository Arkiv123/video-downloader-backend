DMCA & Copyright Policy

Last updated: July 2026

GOOGLY RANKS ("the Service") respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects its users to do the same. The Service is a technical tool that reads publicly available media metadata and helps users retrieve content they own or are otherwise authorized to download. We do not host, store, or upload any third-party media on our servers as a library, and we do not encourage or condone copyright infringement.

1. Reporting copyright infringement

If you are a copyright owner (or an agent authorized to act on their behalf) and you believe that the Service is being used to facilitate infringement of your work, you may submit a written notice containing the following (as required under 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)):

  1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed.
  3. Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing, and information reasonably sufficient to locate it (e.g. the specific URL or request involved).
  4. Your contact information — name, address, telephone number, and email.
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the owner or are authorized to act on the owner's behalf.

2. What happens after a valid notice

Upon receiving a valid notice, we will act expeditiously to disable access to, or remove the ability to process, the identified material where it is within our technical control. Because the Service does not maintain a hosted media library, remediation typically means blocking the relevant source pattern from being processed.

3. Counter-notice

If you believe material was disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice to the same contact address with the information required under 17 U.S.C. § 512(g).

4. Repeat infringers

We reserve the right to block access for any user who is the subject of repeat, valid infringement notices.

5. No legal advice

This page describes our process; it is not legal advice. If you are unsure of your rights, consult a qualified attorney.
